# Senate ↔ RLM-Creative (The Garland)

The Muses create; Minerva clears what they may publish. The Garland
already has an in-crew IP gate — `governance-c2pa` (Helios's sub-crew,
HITL on IP-clearance failure). The Senate is the depth behind that gate.

## Escalation path

`governance-c2pa` flags an asset → if the question exceeds its checklist
(genuine clearance analysis needed), escalate to the Senate:
`/ip-clearance <asset + provenance> --type asset` (Minerva), or full
`/senate` for multi-issue campaigns (IP + claims + privacy in one
brief).

## What Minerva checks on creative assets

- Third-party marks, characters, music, recognizable persons in frame.
- Reference-material provenance and style-imitation risk (living
  artists).
- Stock-license scope: territory, resolution, commercial use,
  AI-derivative terms.
- Model releases; C2PA signing prerequisites.

## What the broader Curia adds for campaigns

- **Claims review** — substantiation for performance/comparative claims
  (Ulpian's regime knowledge: FTC-style endorsement/disclosure rules,
  sector advertising regimes) — complements MarketBliss's
  brand-safety-compliance (see `integrations/marketbliss.md`).
- **Privacy** — Angerona on UGC campaigns, contest data, tracking-pixel
  questions.
- **Contracts** — Gaius on talent agreements, agency paper, licensing
  deals.

## Envelope note

The Senate accepts `CREATIVE_BRIEF` envelopes precisely for this seam: a
brief routed to legal-compliance gets clearance + claims counsel as a
DECISION_RECORD the Garland can attach to its own gate record.

## Mythological seam

The Muses sing; Minerva — born armed, patron of every artisan — decides
what may bear the maker's mark. C2PA is just provenance-signing; Minerva
is *clearance*: the difference between proving who made it and proving
they were allowed to.
