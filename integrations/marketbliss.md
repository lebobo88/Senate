# Senate ↔ MarketBliss (Marketing Operations)

MarketBliss runs marketing operations across five squads with its own
brand-safety-compliance gatekeeper. That gatekeeper handles brand policy;
the Senate is its **legal escalation target** for regulated-claims and
regime questions.

## Escalation triggers (MarketBliss → Senate)

- **Regulated claims** — health, financial, environmental ("green"),
  comparative, or performance claims needing substantiation analysis →
  `/legal-opinion regulatory-counsel <claim + substantiation file>` or
  full `/senate` for campaign-wide review.
- **Regime-bound channels** — email/SMS consent regimes, sweepstakes/
  contest law, influencer disclosure rules → Ulpian.
- **Data-driven targeting** — audience data lawfulness, lookalike
  modeling on personal data, tracking consent → Angerona
  (`/privacy-assess`).
- **Trademark adjacency** — competitor-name usage, comparative
  advertising marks → Minerva.

The `regulated-health-finance` industry profile in MarketBliss should
treat Senate review as a standing gate for claim-bearing assets.

## Return contract

The Senate returns a DECISION_RECORD with: claim-by-claim verdicts
(supported / needs-substantiation / barred), required disclosures
drafted, and regime citations (verified per Table III). MarketBliss
attaches it to the campaign record; HITL-grade findings ride the
Tribune's Veto.

## Boundary

MarketBliss owns marketing judgment (what converts); ExecutiveSuite's CMO
owns marketing strategy; the Senate owns only the lawfulness layer. A
legally clean claim can still be off-brand — not the Senate's call.
